I. Overview
1. Introduction
The 
Hotel Management System (HMS)
 is a software project designed to provide a complete digital platform for managing the daily operations of a 4-star hotel. The main purpose of this project is to replace many manual hotel management processes with a more organized, efficient, and user-friendly system. Through this platform, the hotel can manage reservations, room availability, guest information, housekeeping activities, service requests, billing, payments, and reports in one centralized system.
The platform supports multiple types of users, including
 Receptionists, Staff, Managers, and Admins
. Each actor has specific functions based on their role in the hotel. Guests can register an account, log in, search for available rooms, view room details, make online reservations, cancel bookings, request additional services, report room issues, view their current bill, and submit reviews after their stay. This helps improve the guest experience by allowing customers to interact with the hotel more easily through an online system.
For hotel employees, the system provides useful tools to support daily work. Receptionists can manage booking lists, create walk-in reservations, modify or cancel reservations, check guests in and out, generate invoices, process payments, add extra charges, and view real-time room status. Staff members, especially housekeeping employees, can view assigned tasks, update room cleaning status, report room issues, and process guest service requests. These features help hotel operations become faster, clearer, and easier to track.
The platform also provides management and administration features. Managers can manage room information, room types, room pricing, maintenance blocks, housekeeping assignments, staff task progress, occupancy reports, revenue summaries, and reservation overviews. Admins can manage internal user accounts, configure system settings, view system activity logs, manage service and amenity catalogs, and access full revenue reports for higher-level decision-making.
Overall, the HMS platform is capable of supporting both 
customer-facing services
 and 
internal hotel operations
. It improves communication between guests and hotel staff, reduces manual paperwork, keeps room and booking information updated in real time, and provides useful reports for monitoring hotel performance. With its use case–driven design, this project creates a clear foundation for further system analysis, design diagrams, database design, and future software development.

3. Entity Relationship Diagram
3.1 Conceptual ERD (Chen Notation) 
                                    
Entities Description
#
Entity
Description
1
ROLE 
Represents the permission group assigned to a user. Each role determines the user's access rights within the hotel management system, such as Guest, Receptionist, Housekeeping Staff, Maintenance Staff, and Manager.
2
USER 
Represents all users of the hotel management system. A user can make reservations, submit feedback, and perform system functions according to their assigned role.
3
RESERVATION 
Represents a room booking made by a user. It stores reservation information such as check-in/check-out dates, booking status, reserved room, requested services, and generated invoice.
4
ROOM 
Represents an individual hotel room available for reservation. Each room belongs to a room type and can be reserved by different reservations over time.
5
ROOM_TYPE
 
Represents a category of rooms with common characteristics such as room size, capacity, facilities, and standard pricing. Multiple rooms may belong to the same room type.
6
SERVICE
 
Represents additional hotel services available to guests during their stay, such as airport transfer, spa, laundry, restaurant, or room service. Services can be requested during a reservation.
7
INVOICE
Represents the billing information generated for a reservation. It summarizes room charges, requested services, taxes, and other applicable fees.
8
PAYMENT 
 
Represents a payment transaction made for an invoice. It records payment information including payment method, amount, transaction status, and payment time. Multiple payment records may exist if partial payments are supported.
9
FEEDBACK 
Represents reviews or comments submitted by users after their stay. Feedback is used to evaluate service quality and improve customer satisfaction.
